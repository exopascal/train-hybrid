# Protein Anchors — The Structural Base of Meals

Protein anchors are **not a diet strategy**.
They are a **structural tool**.

A protein anchor is the part of a meal that provides
a clear protein source and gives the meal stability.

Everything else in the meal can remain flexible.

---

## What a protein anchor is

A protein anchor is:
- a clearly identifiable protein source
- the starting point of a meal
- independent of cuisine, culture, or diet label

It does not define:
- calories
- macros
- portion sizes
- “good” or “bad” foods

---

## Why protein anchors matter

Protein anchors help to:
- support muscle maintenance
- increase satiety
- reduce random snacking
- bring structure without restriction

They make meals more **robust**, not more rigid.

---

## Protein anchors are neutral

The system does **not** rank protein sources.

There is no hierarchy between:
- animal or plant-based
- lean or fattier options
- simple or elaborate meals

The only question is:
> *Is there a clear protein base in the meal?*
