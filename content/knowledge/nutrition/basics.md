# Nutrition Basics — Structure over Rules

This system does not prescribe a specific diet.

There are no forbidden foods, no labels, and no rigid plans.
Instead, the goal is to build **nutritional structure** that works within
real life, personal preferences, and everyday habits.

Nutrition should be **supportive**, not restrictive.

---

## The core idea

Good nutrition is not about perfection.
It is about **consistency and structure**.

A simple and flexible framework is:

- Protein as a stable foundation
- Fats as a conscious energy and flavor source
- Carbohydrates chosen freely based on preference

This framework works with many eating styles and does not require
eliminating foods people enjoy.

---

## Protein as an anchor

Protein plays a special role in everyday nutrition.

It:
- supports muscle maintenance
- increases satiety
- stabilizes meals

Instead of changing everything at once, it is often enough to:
> **Start meals with a clear protein source.**

What that protein source is can vary:
- animal or plant-based
- warm or cold
- simple or elaborate

The system does not rank or moralize protein sources.

---

## Fats are not the enemy

Fats are:
- a natural part of food
- important for flavor and satisfaction
- a dense energy source

They do not need to be avoided.
They benefit from **awareness**, not restriction.

The focus is not on eliminating fats,
but on understanding that they influence:
- energy intake
- satiety
- meal density
